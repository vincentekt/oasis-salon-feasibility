Oasis Hair Salon

Taichung Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: 7th Redevelopment Zone (Xitun) Model: Grand Luxury Flagship (MVP) Currency: USD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Boutique MVP)
Setup Size 800 sq ft
Initial CAPEX USD 90k - 110k
Monthly OPEX USD 14,000
Breakeven Volume 4.7 Customers / Day
Average Ticket USD 110

Main Success Condition: Taichung is driven by "Face" (Mianzi) and prestige. Success requires premium branding, exceptional customer pampering, dedicated styling bays, and high-end services in a high-prestige district like West or Xitun District.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: The most prestigious and private high-end boutique hair salon in West/Xitun District, Taichung.

Avoid being positioned as: A standard, commercial mass-market hair salon or low-end hair spa.

"Masterful hair styling. The peak of personal luxury."

Core Customer Promise: Dedicated personal styling station, expert Director-level hair design, luxury Japanese-inspired hair wellness, and exquisite individual attention.

Market Fit: Taichung's premium segment is willing to pay high prices for top styling talent and prestige. Providing exclusive styling stations, private consultation, and high-performance services creates a powerful market differentiator.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: High concentration of wealthy business owners, particularly in Xitun (7th Zone) and Nantun.
  • Spending Power: The local upper class has immense discretionary income, often outspending Taipei counterparts on luxury cars and wellness.
  • Space & Infrastructure: People drive everywhere. If a business doesn't have private parking, it loses the wealthy demographic.
  • Cultural Drivers: "Bigger is better" is the Taichung business motto. Grand lobbies, high ceilings, and extravagant decor are expected baseline features for premium pricing.

10. Customer Journey & Sales Flow

  • Arrival: Pull into reserved parking. Greeted by a concierge holding an umbrella if raining.
  • Lobby: Double-height ceilings, chandelier, welcome champagne or premium tea.
  • Consultation: Private booth. Scalp analysis using high-end diagnostic tools.
  • Service: Escorted to personal styling station with individual partitions. Reclining wash bed for Japanese scalp massage.
  • Post-Service: Blowout and styling at the same station. Premium finish using top products.
  • Payment: In-suite payment terminal. Pitch the $2,500 Black Card.

17. Marketing Strategy

B2B & Partnerships: Partner with local Porsche/Ferrari dealerships and high-end golf clubs. Gift their top clients VIP Black Cards pre-loaded with a free session.

Visual Impact: Ensure the storefront is lit up like a jewel box at night. Taichung thrives on visible, physical presence.

18. Key Risks & Controls

Risk Type Severity Mitigation / Control
High Fixed CAPEX High Pre-sell VIP Black cards during the final month of renovation to recoup 20-30% of CAPEX immediately.
Underutilization Medium Do not overbuild. 4 styling stations is the maximum for phase 1.
Service Inconsistency High Wealthy clients expect perfection. Maintain strict QA and fire fast if attitudes don't align with luxury standards.

19. Final Recommendation

Decision: GO (Boutique MVP)

  • Location: Xitun (7th Zone) or West District. Must have parking.
  • Setup: 4 Styling Stations, 2 Backwash beds (800 sq ft).
  • Budget Ceiling: USD 110k.
  • Focus: Focus on superior hair design, Director-level styling, and Japanese head care in an intimate boutique setting.

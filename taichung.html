2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: The most prestigious and private high-end boutique hair salon in West/Xitun District, Taichung.

Avoid being positioned as: A standard, commercial mass-market hair salon or low-end hair spa.

"Masterful hair styling. The peak of personal luxury."

Core Customer Promise: Dedicated personal styling station, expert Director-level hair design, luxury Japanese-inspired hair wellness, and exquisite individual attention.

Market Fit: Taichung's premium segment is willing to pay high prices for top styling talent and prestige. Providing exclusive styling stations, private consultation, and high-performance services creates a powerful market differentiator.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Taichung (2.8 million, Taiwan's third-largest city) has a large Japanese expatriate community (~3,000), growing Western tech/manufacturing expat segment, and a famously fashion-forward local creative class. The Qi-qi (七期) luxury development zone has transformed central Taichung into one of Taiwan's most affluent urban environments outside Taipei — with luxury hotel brands (The Lin, Kimpton, W Taichung) anchoring a genuine HNWI residential cluster.
  • Spending Power: Qi-qi zone residents and Taichung professionals budget TWD 4,000–9,000 for premium color. Japanese corporate expats WTP: TWD 6,000–12,000. Creative industries demographic (ages 25–40) budget TWD 3,500–7,000 for specialist creative color. Overall premium market is significantly more developed than Kaohsiung or Tainan.
  • Water Quality: Taichung Water Bureau (TWDB) supply from Deji Reservoir has TDS 80–150 ppm — relatively benign for color services. The USP is technique quality, bilingual service, and boutique experience rather than water filtration.
  • Competitive Environment: ELF, VS Hair, and Mpalace hold the premium segment. All focus on the broader Xitun corridor without a specific Qi-qi zone boutique. The creative balayage specialist and Japanese-bilingual gaps are both genuine commercial blind spots.
  • Regulatory Context: Standard Taiwan BOFT registration. Cosmetology license (美容師執照) required for all chemical service practitioners. 100% foreign ownership permissible. Taichung City Government's Creative Industry Development Zone (Qi-qi area) may offer commercial development incentives for premium service businesses — worth investigating with TEEMA.
  • Foreign Corporate Structure: 100% foreign ownership is permitted but requires pre-approval from the Investment Commission (MOEAIC) and capital verification.
  • Legality & Dispute Resolution: Strong commercial asset protection. Profit remittance to non-resident shareholders is subject to a 21% dividend withholding tax (WHT) unless reduced under double taxation treaties.

10. Customer Journey & Sales Flow

  • Arrival: Pull into reserved parking. Greeted by a concierge holding an umbrella if raining.
  • Lobby: Double-height ceilings, chandelier, welcome champagne or premium tea.
  • Consultation: Private booth. Scalp analysis using high-end diagnostic tools.
  • Service: Escorted to personal styling station with individual partitions. Reclining styling chair for Japanese scalp massage.
  • Post-Service: Blowout and styling at the same station. Premium finish using top products.
  • Payment: In-suite payment terminal. Pitch the $2,500 Black Card.

18. Key Risks & Controls

Risk Type Severity Mitigation / Control
High Fixed CAPEX High Pre-sell VIP Black cards during the final month of renovation to recoup 20-30% of CAPEX immediately.
Underutilization Medium Do not overbuild. 4 styling stations is the maximum for phase 1.
Service Inconsistency High Wealthy clients expect perfection. Maintain strict QA and fire fast if attitudes don't align with luxury standards.
MOEAIC Approval Delays & Dividend Withholding Tax Medium Setup delays from Investment Commission approvals and 21% WHT on remitted profits. Mitigation: Appoint a local accountant to optimize corporate filings and WHT structures.

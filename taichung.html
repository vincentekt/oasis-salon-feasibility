Oasis Hair Salon

Taichung Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: 7th Redevelopment Zone (Xitun) Model: Grand Luxury Flagship (MVP) Currency: USD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Luxury PoC)
Setup Size 1,400 sq ft
Initial CAPEX USD 150k - 180k
Monthly OPEX USD 19,500
Breakeven Volume 7 Customers / Day
Average Ticket USD 100

Main Success Condition: Taichung is driven by "Face" (Mianzi) and grand scale. Success requires a visually stunning, massive storefront, dedicated VIP parking, and palatial interior design. Small, hidden boutique setups (like in Taipei) do not perform as well here.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: The most luxurious, exclusive scalp and hair wellness resort in Central Taiwan.

Avoid being positioned as: A standard, small-scale salon or a fast-turnover service.

"Palatial relaxation. The ultimate wellness destination."

Core Customer Promise: VIP treatment from the moment you park your car. Massive private suites, ultra-premium products, and zero rushing.

Market Fit: Taichung holds immense hidden wealth (manufacturing bosses, real estate tycoons). They prefer to spend on visible luxury and grand experiences. Restaurant and salon sizes in Taichung are famously double the size of Taipei's.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: High concentration of wealthy business owners, particularly in Xitun (7th Zone) and Nantun.
  • Spending Power: The local upper class has immense discretionary income, often outspending Taipei counterparts on luxury cars and wellness.
  • Space & Infrastructure: People drive everywhere. If a business doesn't have private parking, it loses the wealthy demographic.
  • Cultural Drivers: "Bigger is better" is the Taichung business motto. Grand lobbies, high ceilings, and extravagant decor are expected baseline features for premium pricing.

10. Customer Journey & Sales Flow

  • Arrival: Pull into reserved parking. Greeted by a concierge holding an umbrella if raining.
  • Lobby: Double-height ceilings, chandelier, welcome champagne or premium tea.
  • Consultation: Private booth. Scalp analysis using high-end diagnostic tools.
  • Service: Escorted to massive private suite. Features private bathroom, changing area, and Japanese flat-bed.
  • Post-Service: In-suite blowout and styling. No need to move to a public area.
  • Payment: In-suite payment terminal. Pitch the $3,100 Black Card.

17. Marketing Strategy

B2B & Partnerships: Partner with local Porsche/Ferrari dealerships and high-end golf clubs. Gift their top clients VIP Black Cards pre-loaded with a free session.

Visual Impact: Ensure the storefront is lit up like a jewel box at night. Taichung thrives on visible, physical presence.

18. Key Risks & Controls

Risk Type Severity Mitigation / Control
High Fixed CAPEX High Pre-sell VIP Black cards during the final month of renovation to recoup 20-30% of CAPEX immediately.
Underutilization Medium Do not overbuild. 8-10 beds is the maximum for phase 1.
Service Inconsistency High Wealthy clients expect perfection. Maintain strict QA and fire fast if attitudes don't align with luxury standards.

19. Final Recommendation

Decision: GO (Luxury Flagship)

  • Location: Xitun (7th Zone) or Nantun. Must have parking.
  • Setup: 4 Suites, 1,400 sq ft.
  • Budget Ceiling: USD 200k.
  • Focus: Taichung is highly profitable due to lower rents and high ticket prices, but you must invest heavily upfront to win the "Face" war.

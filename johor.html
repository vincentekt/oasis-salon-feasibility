Oasis Hair Salon

Johor Bahru (RTS area) Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: Bukit Chagar RTS Station / JB Sentral Model: Cross-Border PoC / MVP Currency: USD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Cross-Border PoC)
Setup Size 1,000 sq ft
Initial CAPEX USD 70k - 85k
Monthly OPEX USD 8,500
Breakeven Volume 7 Customers / Day
Average Ticket USD 45

Main Success Condition: Establishing the PoC directly adjacent to the Bukit Chagar RTS terminal (e.g. R&F Mall or Zenith Mall) to tap into Singaporean weekend day-trippers seeking high-end Japanese relaxation services at a 70% discount compared to Singapore local prices.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: A premium, Japanese-standard scalp sanctuary offering top-tier carbonated water scalp therapy and deep relaxation. It is specifically designed to meet the high service quality standards expected by Singaporeans while offering pricing that is extremely competitive.

Avoid being positioned as: A low-cost local hair salon, an express haircut joint, or a general full-body massage parlor.

"Singaporean luxury, Malaysian prices. Reclaim your scalp wellness in 60 minutes."

Core Customer Promise: Genuine Japanese-style "Omotenashi" service, imported professional products, and immediate, camera-proven scalp purification at a fraction of Singaporean retail rates.

Market Fit: With the upcoming Johor Bahru-Singapore Rapid Transit System (RTS Link), cross-border traffic will spike. Singaporeans already travel to Johor Bahru for food, dental care, and beauty treatments due to the favorable exchange rate. A specialized, luxurious head spa directly connected to the RTS terminal fills a major market gap.

3. Market Context

  • Cross-Border Demographics: Massive daily flow of Malaysian commuters and weekend rush of Singaporean tourists. Over 300,000 people cross the border daily.
  • Exchange Rate Advantage: Singapore's high currency value makes Malaysian services incredibly cheap for Singaporeans (approx. 1:3.4 SGD to MYR).
  • Market Gap: JB has plenty of massage parlors (foot/body) and standard hair salons, but lacks premium, specialized head spas focusing on starlight quiet sensory relaxation.
  • Travel Convenience: Locations directly connected to the RTS Link allow Singaporeans to visit, receive a premium treatment, shop, and return to Singapore without needing a car or stuck in causeway traffic.
  • Regulatory Context: Favorable commercial environment in Malaysia. Easy company setup, lower payroll costs, and standard beauty salon licensing apply.

10. Customer Journey & Sales Flow

  • Booking: Seamless booking via Whatsapp or online website (synced with Singapore timezone).
  • Arrival: Cross the RTS link, walk 2 minutes to the shop. Greeted with cold hojicha tea.
  • Consultation: Macro scalp camera scan identifies dry/oily zones.
  • Treatment: Escorted to the private dark room. flat-bed wash, carbonated waterfall ring, heated head massage.
  • Styling: Premium styling and blow-dry bar.
  • Review & Checkout: Re-scan scalp to verify results. Suggest the 5-session package card.

17. Marketing Strategy

Singapore Targeting: Target Meta/Instagram ads at users in Singapore (Woodlands, Yishun) who frequently visit Johor Bahru. Focus on "Singapore quality at 1/3 of the price".

Xiaohongshu (Red Book): Leverage travel influencers who review "JB day trips" to feature the head spa as the ultimate relaxation stop before returning to Singapore.

Walk-In Transit Signage: Place prominent signboards at the RTS Link exit walkway pointing to the spa. Offer "RTS Transit Trials" to capture footfall.

18. Key Risks & Controls

Risk Type Severity Mitigation / Control
RTS Completion Delays Medium Rely on existing CIQ causeway traffic and local JB professionals during the bridge completion phase.

Staff Commute / Resignations High Pay above-market rates in JB (MYR 3,000+ base for stylists) to prevent staff from leaving for Singapore jobs.

19. Final Recommendation

Decision: GO (Cross-Border PoC)

  • Location: Johor Bahru (RTS adjacent).
  • Setup: 4 styling chairs, 1,000 sq ft.
  • Budget: USD 80k CAPEX, USD 8.5k OPEX.
  • Expansion Trigger: If monthly revenues exceed USD 20k for 4 consecutive months, plan a second outlet in Mid Valley Southkey or Bukit Indah.

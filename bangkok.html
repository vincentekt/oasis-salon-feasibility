Oasis Hair Salon

Bangkok Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: Thonglor / Ekkamai Model: Premium Boutique / PoC Currency: USD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Expat PoC)

Initial CAPEX USD 110k - 130k
Monthly OPEX USD 13,000
Breakeven Volume 5.3 Customers / Day
Average Ticket USD 90

Main Success Condition: Leasing an upstairs commercial suite or a quiet side-street shoplot in the trendy Thonglor expat district, targeting Japanese/Western corporate expats and affluent Thai professionals who frequent upscale wellness clinics. High-quality Japanese-spec OEM equipment keeps initial setup costs lean.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: A premium boutique hair salon specializing in advanced color techniques (balayage, highlights), precision cuts, and luxury scalp-hair therapy. It should operate in upscale, high-visibility spaces to capture high-value creative color clients while optimizing lease viability. Focuses on premium service quality and expert styling.

Avoid being positioned as: A standard high-volume discount hair salon, or a purely therapeutic head spa. It must feel like a premium creative design studio.

"Your signature look. Expert styling, premium care."

Core Customer Promise: Artistic hair transformation, high-end coloring, and deep scalp rejuvenation in a premium aesthetic environment.

Market Fit: Surging demand for premium chemical work (balayage/highlights) and high-quality international styling among affluent professionals and expat communities. While Bangkok has high-end hair salons, they often lack specialized scalp care integration and operate in crowded open layouts. We fill the gap by combining elite creative styling with luxury scalp care in semi-private comfort.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Over 150,000 expats in Bangkok, with a heavy concentration of Japanese families in Thonglor and Ekkamai.
  • Spending Power: High disposable incomes among international professionals and corporate directors. Routine budget for aesthetics.
  • Environmental Drivers: Intense city humidity and seasonal air pollution (PM2.5) lead to severe scalp oiliness, oil clog, and styling needs.
  • Competitive Environment: Dozens of premium Japanese hair salons, but none focused solely on private, acoustic head spa sanctuaries.
  • Licensing: Standard hair salon permits cover all operations, ensuring simple setup.

10. Customer Journey

  1. Booking: Online via Instagram/Whatsapp with automated slot booking.
  2. Welcome: Arrive in our cozy botanical lobby, served warm ginger tea.
  3. Analysis: Microscope check displays scalp health to the customer.
  4. Treatment: Transition to private starlight VIP pod for carbonated therapy.
  5. Blowdry & Close: Post-treatment comparison scan, blowout styling, package promotion.

11. Setup & Layout

The 800 sq ft space is optimized for visual privacy and client flow:

  • Lobby & Retail Zone (150 sqft): Elegant reception desk, product shelves displaying premium organic haircare lines.
  • 4 Styling Station Bays (400 sqft): Semi-private bays, professional track lighting, styling vanity, 4 styling chairs.
  • Backwash Station (150 sqft): 2 luxury backwash beds with carbonated hair rinse systems and dim ambient lighting.
  • Color Lab & Utility (100 sqft): Color mixing inventory, water heater, carbonated system generators, laundry.

12. Staffing Model

Bangkok's moderate labor costs support an efficient operations budget:

  • 1 Master Stylist / Director: USD 2,500/month (leads creative work, manages bookings, customer experience).
  • 2 Senior Stylists: USD 1,500/month base salary each (expert coloring, cut, and scalp-hair fusion therapy).
  • 1 Stylist Assistant: USD 800/month base salary (assists with shampoos, head massages, styling preparation, laundry).
Incentive Model: 10% commission sharing on membership package sales to motivate stylists and maximize retention.

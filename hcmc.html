Oasis Hair Salon

Ho Chi Minh City Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: District 1 / District 3 Model: Expat Salon PoC / MVP Currency: USD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Premium Salon MVP)

Monthly OPEX USD 10,700
Breakeven Volume ~4.2 Customers / Day
Average Ticket USD 95

Main Success Condition: Establishing the salon in District 1/3 or Thao Dien to capture affluent Vietnamese professionals, Japanese/Korean/Western expat executives and their spouses near key retail and business districts. The focus is to build a prestige-based relationship with high-margin creative color work, premium cuts, and advanced hair therapies, supported by high-retention package models.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: A premium boutique hair salon specializing in advanced color techniques (balayage, highlight), precision cuts, and luxury scalp-hair therapy. It operates in upscale, high-visibility ground floor retail storefronts in District 1/3 to capture high-value creative color clients while optimizing lease viability.

Avoid being positioned as: A standard high-volume discount hair salon, or a purely therapeutic scalp clinic. It must feel like a premium creative design studio.

"Your signature look. Expert styling, soft-water hair care."

Core Customer Promise: Artistic hair transformation, high-end coloring, and deep scalp rejuvenation in a premium aesthetic environment.

Market Fit: Ho Chi Minh City has a rapidly growing class of affluent locals and expats who demand international styling standards, damage-free organic dyes, and specialized scalp treatments. Existing options are divided between low-end local shops or expensive traditional salons that lack specialized, integrated scalp-care wellness systems.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Concentrated in District 1, District 3, and neighboring District 2 (Thao Dien). Large expat bases and affluent locals.
  • Spending Power: Expats and affluent Vietnamese earn USD 2,500+ monthly and allocate significant budget to high-end hair design, color maintenance, and cosmetic treatments.
  • Environmental Drivers: Dust and high tropical humidity in HCMC cause oily scalp and damaged hair, necessitating combined hair styling and scalp deep cleansing.
  • Water Quality: Hard municipal water with heavy mineral contents causes hair color to fade quickly and irritates the scalp, making salon-grade soft water filtration and detox rinse a premium competitive advantage.
  • Regulatory Context: Salons operate under standard retail/service licensing.

10. Customer Journey & Sales Flow

  • Discovery: Expats find Oasis on Google Maps or via recommendations in local Facebook/WhatsApp groups.
  • Reception: Welcome with organic tea and warm towel in the lobby area.
  • Consultation: Scalp micro-scan shows oily build-up and clogged pores on tablet screen.
  • Service: Escorted to a semi-private styling bay. Flat-bed shampoo treatment with steam halo, scalp massage, and custom rinse.
  • Blow-dry: Precision cut and styling blowout with Dyson dryers. Second scan shows clear, healthy hair follicles.
  • Checkout: Stylist presents the pre-paid package offer based on the visible before/after scan results.

17. Marketing Strategy

Expat Communities: Partner with local expat Facebook groups and post organic, helpful scalp health guides. Offer a 20% discount code for group members.

Google Maps / Local SEO: Ensure Google Business Profile is fully set up, translations are accurate, and immediately encourage happy soft-launch customers to leave 5-star English reviews.

Aesthetic Styling Reels: Post high-quality video reels of before/after hair transformations, balayage techniques, and relaxing scalp-hair fusion treatments on Instagram and local expat groups.

Oasis Hair Salon

Taipei Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: Da'an / Xinyi District Model: Premium Boutique Salon (MVP) Currency: USD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Premium Salon PoC)

Initial CAPEX USD 100k - 120k
Monthly OPEX USD 13,000
Breakeven Volume 4 Customers / Day
Average Ticket USD 120

Main Success Condition: High aesthetic appeal and specialized premium services are critical. Taipei consumers are extremely design-conscious and respond well to modern, minimalist layouts. Success relies on high-quality styling execution (precision cuts, balayage) paired with relaxing hair salon backwash treatments in Da'an or Xinyi alleys, and influencer-led word of mouth.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: A premium, boutique hair salon offering a hybrid experience of high-end styling (precision cuts, custom balayage) and deeply relaxing Japanese-style backwash scalp therapies.

Avoid being positioned as: A mass-market discount salon chain or a clinical scalp-treatment center.

"Quiet luxury. Flawless style, restored wellness."

Core Customer Promise: Exceptional styling execution combined with a relaxing Japanese backwash scalp ritual in a premium, minimalist environment.

Market Fit: Taipei's high-income consumers in Da'an and Xinyi demand top-tier hair styling but increasingly seek mental relaxation and privacy. We bridge the gap between high-traffic celebrity salons and low-capacity private studios.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Taipei has ~30,000–40,000 Western expats plus 15,000+ Japanese residents. The core premium salon market clusters in Da'an District (Daan Park area), Zhongshan, and Xinyi. TSMC supply chain engineers, tech industry expats (Google, Meta, LINE Taiwan), and AIT/diplomatic families represent the primary high-income target segments.
  • Spending Power: Tech industry expats and senior Taiwanese professionals have WTP of TWD 5,000–12,000 per color session. Da'an area average household income is among the highest in Taiwan. Fashion-forward young professionals budget TWD 3,500–7,000.
  • Water Quality: Taipei Water Department provides treated tap water with TDS ~100–150 ppm — moderate quality. Northern Taiwan's soft mountain water means mineral buildup is less severe than tropical cities. USP centers on technique, time efficiency, and bilingual expertise rather than water filtration.
  • Competitive Environment: Eddie Tham (solo), SeeFu, Incircle, and A.People collectively serve the premium expat color market. The combined capacity bottleneck (3–4 week waits for Eddie Tham, 6–9 hour session times at Incircle) means unmet demand is structural, not marginal.
  • Regulatory Context: Standard Taiwan BOFT business registration. Cosmetology license (美容師執照) required for all styling staff performing chemical services. No foreign ownership restrictions — 100% foreign-owned company permissible via standard company law.

10. Customer Journey & Sales Flow

  • Discovery: High-end aesthetic photos on Instagram and Threads.
  • Arrival: Remove shoes, put on slippers. Served cold brew tea or warm hojicha.
  • Consultation: Macro-lens scalp analysis. Explanation of clogged pores vs healthy pores.
  • Service: Transition to completely dark, soundproofed room. Heated blanket. Waterfall ring (hair salon halo).
  • Post-Service: Dyson blowout. Second hair consultation to show perfectly clean follicles.
  • Payment: Soft-sell the 6-session card based on the visual "before/after".

17. Marketing Strategy

Platform Focus: Instagram and Threads. Threads is highly active in Taiwan for authentic reviews; Instagram for aesthetic appeal.

Influencer Seeding: Gift sessions to micro-influencers (10k-30k followers) in the fashion/lifestyle niche.

Visual Proof: Heavily feature the "Scalp Camera Before/After" shots in ads. Taiwanese consumers respond well to scientific proof of cleanliness.

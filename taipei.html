Oasis Hair Salon

Taipei Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: Da'an / Xinyi District Model: Premium Boutique Salon (MVP) Currency: USD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Premium Salon PoC)
Setup Size 850 sq ft
Initial CAPEX USD 100k - 120k
Monthly OPEX USD 13,000
Breakeven Volume 4 Customers / Day
Average Ticket USD 120

Main Success Condition: High aesthetic appeal and specialized premium services are critical. Taipei consumers are extremely design-conscious and respond well to modern, minimalist layouts. Success relies on high-quality styling execution (precision cuts, balayage) paired with relaxing head spa backwash treatments in Da'an or Xinyi alleys, and influencer-led word of mouth.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: A premium, boutique hair salon offering a hybrid experience of high-end styling (precision cuts, custom balayage) and deeply relaxing Japanese-style backwash scalp therapies.

Avoid being positioned as: A mass-market discount salon chain or a clinical scalp-treatment center.

"Quiet luxury. Flawless style, restored wellness."

Core Customer Promise: Exceptional styling execution combined with a relaxing Japanese backwash scalp ritual in a premium, minimalist environment.

Market Fit: Taipei's high-income consumers in Da'an and Xinyi demand top-tier hair styling but increasingly seek mental relaxation and privacy. We bridge the gap between high-traffic celebrity salons and low-capacity private studios.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Taipei has high median wealth, specifically in Da'an, Xinyi, and Neihu (Tech hubs).
  • Spending Power: The target segment earns USD 3,100+ monthly and spends heavily on coffee, skincare, and boutique fitness.
  • Weather Relevance: Taipei is extremely humid and hot for 8 months of the year, leading to widespread oily scalp and hair flattening issues. Scalp detoxification has huge organic demand.
  • Cultural Drivers: Taiwanese consumers idolize Japanese service standards and aesthetic trends. "Wabi-sabi" and minimalist interior design will attract the wealthy demographic instantly.
  • Regulatory Context: Easy to set up under standard salon licensing. Less restrictive than Hong Kong.

10. Customer Journey & Sales Flow

  • Discovery: High-end aesthetic photos on Instagram and Threads.
  • Arrival: Remove shoes, put on slippers. Served cold brew tea or warm hojicha.
  • Consultation: Macro-lens scalp analysis. Explanation of clogged pores vs healthy pores.
  • Service: Transition to completely dark, soundproofed room. Heated blanket. Waterfall ring (Head Spa halo).
  • Post-Service: Dyson blowout. Second scalp scan to show perfectly clean follicles.
  • Payment: Soft-sell the 6-session card based on the visual "before/after".

17. Marketing Strategy

Platform Focus: Instagram and Threads. Threads is highly active in Taiwan for authentic reviews; Instagram for aesthetic appeal.

Influencer Seeding: Gift sessions to micro-influencers (10k-30k followers) in the fashion/lifestyle niche.

Visual Proof: Heavily feature the "Scalp Camera Before/After" shots in ads. Taiwanese consumers respond well to scientific proof of cleanliness.

18. Key Risks & Controls

Risk Type Severity Mitigation / Control
High Renovation Costs High Get 3 quotes. Avoid excessive structural changes; focus on lighting and furniture to build aesthetics.
Staff Poaching Medium Offer clear career progression to Salon Director and profit-sharing for senior stylists.
Summer Seasonality Low In winter, pivot marketing to "Moisturizing & Warm Deep Sleep" treatments.

19. Final Recommendation

Decision: GO (Boutique Flagship)

  • Location: Da'an District (Quiet alleyway shop, ground floor preferred).
  • Setup: 4 Styling Stations, 2 Backwash Beds, 850 sq ft.
  • Budget Ceiling: USD 120,000.
  • Expansion Trigger: Achieve 80% occupancy on styling stations and USD 40k monthly revenue for 3 consecutive months.

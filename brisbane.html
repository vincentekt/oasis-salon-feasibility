2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: A premium boutique hair salon specializing in advanced color techniques (balayage, highlights), precision cuts, and luxury scalp-hair therapy. Positioned as an elite creative styling studio.

Avoid being positioned as: A standard high-volume discount hair salon, a budget express haircut shop, or a purely therapeutic hair salon.

"Your signature look. Expert styling, soft-water hair care."

Core Customer Promise: Expert hair styling and coloring paired with specialized, restorative scalp treatments that promote hair health and complete relaxation.

Market Fit: Brisbane has a large demographic with high disposable income who spend heavily on wellness, skin clinics, and hair styling. However, there is a distinct gap for premium boutique salons that seamlessly integrate advanced hair styling with specialized scalp-hair health treatments in a tranquil, spacious environment. Existing hair salons treat head care as a brief, rushed wash add-on, while traditional spas cannot offer creative hair styling. Our premium PoC closes this gap.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Brisbane has 100,000+ international students (UQ, QUT) plus 30,000+ skilled visa expats concentrated in inner suburbs (New Farm, Teneriffe, Fortitude Valley, Paddington). Brisbane's population grew by 2.3% in 2023–24 — the fastest of any Australian capital — driven by interstate and international migration. New Farm's median house price exceeds AUD $2.1M, signalling the wealth concentration in the inner-east corridor.
  • Spending Power: Brisbane premium salon clients spend AUD $300–600 for quality balayage. The Korean and Japanese community (growing rapidly in Sunnybank, Robertson, and Fortitude Valley) represents an underserved, high-WTP segment. Post-Olympics investment (2032 Brisbane Olympics) is accelerating luxury retail development in South Bank and the inner-east.
  • Environmental Drivers: Brisbane's UV index (regularly 10–12 in summer) is the most extreme of any city in this 27-city shortlist. Color fades significantly faster, driving demand for UV-protection add-ons and shorter refresh cycles — a commercially exploitable difference vs. temperate-city competitors.
  • Competitive Environment: Sol Hair, Beau Gordon, Elysium, Stefan, and Little Birdie collectively serve the premium segment with under 30 chairs total across Brisbane. None serve the New Farm/Teneriffe inner-east corridor. None specialize in Asian hair types.
  • Regulatory Context: Australian Business Number (ABN) registration. Standard Queensland QBCC/local council commercial premises approval. All color service practitioners must hold a Certificate III in Hairdressing (minimum) — ample supply of qualified stylists from TAFE Queensland training programs.

  2. Lobby Welcome: Welcome tea, brief intake form regarding scalp concerns.
  3. Microscopic Analysis: Scalp check using high-definition lens displayed on screen.

  5. Blowdry & Styling: Professional dry-off, final comparison scan, membership package presentation.

11. Setup & Layout

The 800 sq ft space is optimized for visual flow, sensory relaxation, and strict plumbing efficiency:

  • Reception & Botanical Lounge (150 sqft): Warm lighting, local plant aesthetics, retail product shelving.
  • 4 Semi-Private Styling Stations (400 sqft): Custom styling mirrors, premium lighting, imported Japanese-spec styling chairs.
  • 2 Backwash & Scalp Wash Stations (150 sqft): Advanced backstyling chairs, carbonated water rinse system, quiet head massage setup.
  • Utility & Pump Room (100 sqft): Carbonated water generators, hot water storage, AS/NZS 3500 compliant plumbing manifold.

12. Staffing Model

Staffing models reflect Australian award compliance (MA000005) and standard loaded employer oncosts:

  • 1 Master Stylist / Salon Director: USD 5,800/month loaded (supervises staff, handles premium color bookings, and oversees operations).
  • 2 Senior Stylists: USD 4,000/month loaded each (highly skilled in precision cuts, balayage, and scalp treatments).
  • 1 Stylist Assistant: USD 3,000/month loaded (manages backwash, client prep, and basic blowouts).
Incentive Model: 8% commission on membership package sales to motivate staff and secure high-retention bookings.

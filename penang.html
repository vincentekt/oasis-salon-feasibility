2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: A luxury boutique hair salon offering bespoke precision cuts, Japanese-style styling, organic coloring/balayage, and advanced scalp-hair therapy in a quiet, serene environment. An elegant, high-standard hospitality experience.

Avoid being positioned as: A noisy mass-market mall chain, a generic budget haircut shop, or a fast-paced walk-in discount salon.

"Bespoke Hair Artistry. Elegance on the Penang coast."

Core Customer Promise: Expert styling, safe organic/ammonia-free hair color systems, and exceptional personalized hospitality.

Market Fit: Penang has a high concentration of affluent local families, MM2H expat retirees, and long-stay digital nomads in Gurney Drive and Tanjung Tokong. They demand high-caliber styling and personalized attention, but local offerings are dominated by noisy, open-layout mall chains or low-end shops. Oasis Salon provides a peaceful, premium boutique alternative.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Penang has ~25,000–35,000 foreign residents including Malaysia My Second Home (MM2H) retirees (British, Australian, Dutch heavy), semiconductor industry expats (Intel, Agilent, Motorola campuses in Bayan Lepas), and a significant Japanese expat community (~3,000). Georgetown's heritage zone and Tanjong Bungah are the primary expat residential clusters.
  • Spending Power: MM2H retirees and senior tech executives have high WTP (MYR 400–900 / session). The younger semiconductor expat community spends MYR 250–500. Affluent Penang locals (Burmese Road HNWI, Gurney Drive condos) match expat WTP.
  • Water Quality: Penang Water Supply Corporation (PBAPP) water has moderate TDS (100–200 ppm) — better than KL but still calcium-heavy enough to affect color longevity. Soft-water filtration provides a genuine benefit and differentiator.
  • Competitive Environment: Wave Salon, Hairstory, A-Saloon, and Twiggy are the main players. None operate as a standalone boutique within Georgetown's core expat residential corridor. The gap between Gurney Mall premium and Georgetown's walking-distance boutique is genuine and exploitable.
  • Regulatory Context: SSM business registration. Private salon operates under Ministry of Health salon hygiene code. Skilled Pass (SP) or Employment Pass (EP) required for foreign stylist hires. Halal-sensitive market — private styling options required for Malay Muslim clientele segments.

  2. Welcome: Arrive in our cozy botanical lobby, served warm ginger tea.
  3. Analysis: Microscope check displays scalp health to the customer.
  4. Treatment: Transition to private custom track lighting VIP pod for carbonated therapy.
  5. Blowdry & Close: Post-treatment comparison scan, blowout styling, package promotion.

11. Setup & Layout

The 800 sq ft space is optimized for client privacy and efficient workflow:

  • Lobby & Reception (150 sqft): Cozy lounge seating, tea bar, retail display shelves.
  • Styling & Cut Area (400 sqft): 4 premium acoustic styling stations with dynamic lighting and vanity mirrors.
  • Backwash Station (150 sqft): 2 ergonomic backstyling chairs with Japanese head bath attachments.
  • Storage & Utility (100 sqft): Color mixing lab, laundry, carbonated generators, organic stock.

12. Staffing Model

Stylist salaries in Penang reflect premium local talent rates:

  • 1 Salon Director / Master Stylist: USD 1,200/month base salary (leads styling, manages operations).
  • 2 Senior Stylists: USD 800/month base salary each (handle coloring, cut, styling).
  • 2 Stylist Assistants: USD 500/month base salary each (handle washing, blowouts, sanitation).
Commissions: 10% commission sharing on membership package sales to motivate stylists and maximize retention.

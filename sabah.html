Oasis Hair Salon

Sabah Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: Jesselton Quay / Imago Area Model: Coastal Tourism PoC / MVP Currency: USD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Coastal PoC)
Setup Size 800 sq ft
Initial CAPEX USD 40k - 50k
Monthly OPEX USD 6,000
Breakeven Volume 6 Customers / Day
Average Ticket USD 40

Main Success Condition: Leasing a highly visible waterfront commercial unit near Jesselton Quay or Imago Mall to capture international tourists, luxury cruise passengers, and high-income local families.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: A premium, peaceful scalp treatment sanctuary combining Japanese carbonated water misting, soundproof therapy pods, and micro-camera scalp analytics. A relaxing, multilingual, high-standard hospitality experience.

Avoid being positioned as: A local generic beauty shop, a budget massage center, or a noisy haircut salon.

"Waterfront Scalp Sanctuary. Recharge by the Jesselton waterfront."

Core Customer Promise: Visible improvement in scalp hygiene and deep physical relaxation using premium organic botanicals and Japanese technology.

Market Fit: Sabah attracts an international pool of East Asian tourists (Korea, China, Taiwan) and regional business travellers who seek high-end wellness treatments. While Penang has standard body massage spas and local salons, it lacks dedicated scalp health specialists offering private acoustic pods.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Expats concentrated heavily in Georgetown, Jesselton Quay, Imago Mall, and Likas Bay. Large pool of English/Japanese/Korean speakers.
  • Spending Power: High concentration of wealth due to multinational retirees and business owners. Healthy allocation to leisure and wellness.
  • Environmental Drivers: Humid coastal climate and tropical sun trigger oily scalp and hair fall issues, creating demand for regular deep washes.
  • Competitive Environment: Abundant local beauty shops and wellness massages, but zero specialized scalp therapy clinics.
  • Licensing: Operates under standard salon guidelines, facilitating quick establishment.

10. Customer Journey

  1. Booking: Online via Instagram/Whatsapp with automated slot booking.
  2. Welcome: Arrive in our cozy botanical lobby, served warm ginger tea.
  3. Analysis: Microscope check displays scalp health to the customer.
  4. Treatment: Transition to private starlight VIP pod for carbonated therapy.
  5. Blowdry & Close: Post-treatment comparison scan, blowout styling, package promotion.

11. Setup & Layout

The 800 sq ft space is optimized for visual privacy and client flow:

  • Lobby & Consultation (150 sqft): Coastal botanical themes, retail display shelves.
  • 4 Private VIP Pods (400 sqft): Soundproofed walls, starlight projection ceilings, Japanese styling chairs.
  • Blowdry Station (150 sqft): Compact styling chairs and professional vanity mirrors.
  • Storage & Utility (100 sqft): Carbonated water system generators, laundry, organic stock.

12. Staffing Model

Low local wages in Penang support an efficient operations budget:

  • 1 Salon Director: USD 800/month base salary (manages bookings, guest relations, accounting).
  • 4 Stylists: USD 600/month base salary (trained in Japanese massage styles).
  • 1 Assistant: USD 400/month (handles laundry, cleaning).
Commissions: 10% commission sharing on membership package sales to motivate stylists and maximize retention.

Oasis Hair Salon

Singapore Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: Orchard / Tanjong Pagar Model: Luxury Micro-Spa / PoC Currency: USD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Luxury Micro-Spa)
Setup Size 650 sq ft
Initial CAPEX USD 140k - 165k
Monthly OPEX USD 22,000
Breakeven Volume 5-6 Customers / Day
Average Ticket USD 140

Main Success Condition: Positioning the spa as an "Upstairs Luxury Suite / Ginza-style Hideaway" in an older complex or high-rise office tower (e.g. Far East Plaza, Orchard Plaza) to fit the USD 7,000/mo rent budget (approx. S$10 - S$15 psf) while targeting high-net-worth local professionals and expats. Ground-floor Orchard mall spaces are cost-prohibitive (exceeding S$30 psf). Seamless digital booking and flawless Japanese service standards are critical.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: An ultra-exclusive, private scalp wellness sanctuary utilizing Japanese carbonated mist systems, soundproofed styling stations, and high-definition scalp microscopic analytics. Operated as a Ginza-style upstairs suite to ensure absolute privacy, quiet luxury, and commercial rent efficiency. Focuses on premium luxury hospitality and deep mental relaxation.

Avoid being positioned as: A local mass-market salon, a generic foot massage shop, or a clinical hair restoration clinic.

"Quiet Luxury. Reclaim your stillness in Singapore's core."

Core Customer Promise: Visible scalp restoration and deep sensory relaxation using certified organic botanicals and premium Japanese equipment.

Market Fit: Singapore's elite professionals and expat executives face high-stress schedules, tropical humidity, and scalp oiliness. They are highly willing to pay for premium wellness. While Singapore has high-end hair salons, they prioritize cutting and coloring. We fill the gap for dedicated private head spa luxury.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Over 1.5M high-net-worth individuals and multinational expats. High density of premium wellness consumers.
  • Spending Power: Elite global spending bracket. High willingness-to-pay for premium personal care, hygiene, and mental wellness.
  • Environmental Drivers: Constant year-round heat and humidity trigger scalp sweating, clogging, and sebum buildup.
  • Competitive Environment: Saturated with premium hair salons and luxury hotel spas, but lacks dedicated private acoustic head spa pods.
  • Licensing: Operates under standard salon guidelines, ensuring quick business setup.

10. Customer Journey

  1. Booking: Online via custom mobile app with instant schedule confirmation.
  2. Arrival: Welcomed in a luxury botanical lobby, served warm green tea.
  3. Scalp Analysis: Microscope scanner checks scalp health.
  4. Treatment: Transition to private starlight VIP pod, treated with carbonated water.
  5. Blowdry & Close: Post-treatment comparison scan, styling blowout, repeat booking prompt.

11. Setup & Layout

The 650 sq ft space is optimized for visual privacy and client flow:

  • Lobby & Consultation (150 sqft): Botanical theme, organic product shelves.
  • 3 Private VIP Pods (350 sqft): Soundproofed walls, starlight projection ceilings, Japanese styling chairs.
  • Blowdry Station (100 sqft): Compact styling chairs and professional vanity mirrors.
  • Storage & Utility (50 sqft): Carbonated water system generators, laundry, organic stock.

12. Staffing Model

Singapore's high labor costs require a highly efficient operations budget:

  • 1 Salon Director: USD 3,500/month (handles bookings, guest relations, accounting).
  • 3 Stylist Assistants: USD 2,800/month base salary (Omotenashi hospitality trained).
  • 1 Assistant: USD 1,800/month (handles laundry, cleaning).
Incentive Model: 10% commission sharing on membership package sales to motivate stylists and maximize retention.

  • Google Maps reviews: Proactive campaign targeting digital nomads looking for workspace relaxation.
  • Hotel cross-marketing: Place brochures in premium boutique hotels in Tanjong Pagar.

Oasis Hair Salon

Hanoi Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: Tay Ho (West Lake) / Ba Dinh Model: Expat PoC / MVP Currency: USD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Expat PoC)
Setup Size 800 sq ft
Initial CAPEX USD 30k - 38k
Monthly OPEX USD 7,500
Breakeven Volume 7-8 Customers / Day
Average Ticket USD 38

Main Success Condition: Securing a storefront in the high-end Tay Ho (West Lake) residential block to capture wealthy Western diplomats, Asian expats, and affluent local Vietnamese families. Direct road visibility and dedicated parking are critical to capture returning customers.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: A premium, tranquil scalp sanctuary utilizing Japanese carbonated water therapy, starlight pod acoustics, and micro-camera scalp analytics. A completely stress-free, quiet, English-friendly environment.

Avoid being positioned as: A local low-cost hair wash, a standard hair cutting salon, or an invasive medical clinic.

"Lakeside Scalp Sanctuary. Escape the urban rush, restore your peace."

Core Customer Promise: Visible scalp health transformation and deep mental relaxation using organic imported botanicals and professional Japanese equipment.

Market Fit: Hanoi expats and affluent locals face dry, dusty winters and highly polluted air that lead to scalp irritation. While Hanoi has many local hair salons and generic massage parlors, it lacks specialized, premium head spa sanctuaries that offer a quiet, high-integrity hospitality experience.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Significant expat community of ~30,000, heavily concentrated in Tay Ho (lakeside) and Ciputra. Massive diplomat community in Ba Dinh.
  • Spending Power: High-spending expats and luxury local residential base with disposable income allocated towards premium wellness, aesthetic treatments, and self-care.
  • Environmental Drivers: High seasonal air pollution (particularly PM2.5 in autumn/winter) and hard tap water make regular scalp detoxes a health necessity.
  • Competitive Environment: Intense competition in low-cost hair washes, but zero high-end head spas with proper private pods and specialized scalp care technology.
  • Licensing: Fits under standard hair salon license regulations, avoiding expensive medical clinic compliance.

10. Customer Journey

  1. Booking: Online via Instagram/Zalo with instant automated availability confirmation.
  2. Reception: Expat guests welcomed in a botanical lakeside lobby, served warm herbal tea.
  3. Scalp Analysis: High-definition micro-camera scanning to determine sebum levels and hair health.
  4. Therapy: Transition to private starlight styling stations, treatment with filtered carbonated water.
  5. Closing: Post-treatment scan showing cleaned pores, styling blowout, booking follow-up package.

11. Setup & Layout

The 800 sq ft space will be laid out to optimize privacy and stylist efficiency:

  • Lobby & Consultation Area (150 sqft): Botanical theme, organic retail display.
  • 4 Private VIP Pods (400 sqft): Soundproofed walls, starlight projection ceilings, Japanese styling chairs.
  • Blowdry & Styling Stations (150 sqft): Compact styling chairs and professional mirrors.
  • Staff & Inventory Room (100 sqft): Product mixing, storage, laundry facilities.

12. Staffing Model

Low local wages in Hanoi allow for a highly competitive staffing structure:

  • 1 Salon Director: USD 600/month base salary (responsible for booking coordination, guest relations, English translation).
  • 4 Stylists: USD 400/month base salary (trained in Japanese massage styles).
  • 1 Cleaning/Receptionist: USD 300/month base salary.
Profit Sharing incentive: 10% of package commissions are distributed directly to stylists to ensure service quality and customer retention.

To win local expat trust, we will deploy a targeted outreach campaign:

  • Japanese/Korean Expat outreach: Run Japanese language ads on LINE and target local relocation communities.
  • Influencer Campaign: Invite local aesthetic and lifestyle vloggers for complimentary sessions in exchange for video walkthroughs.

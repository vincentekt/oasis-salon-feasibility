Oasis Hair Salon

Perth Market Entry & Feasibility Study

Target: Subiaco / Perth CBD Model: Ground-Floor Premium Salon (MVP) Currency: USD ($) / AUD ($)

1. Executive Summary

Recommendation Proceed (Premium PoC)

Average Ticket USD 180 (AUD 270)

Main Success Condition: Establishing our premium boutique hair salon in a highly visible lifestyle hub in Perth (targeting Subiaco / Perth CBD) to capture affluent urban professionals, high-income retirees, and style-conscious residents. Establishing a ground-floor boutique layout with advanced water softening systems mitigates high Australian setup costs, securing a high-margin premium client base.

2. Business Positioning

Positioned as: A premium ground-floor boutique hair salon specializing in bespoke coloring/balayage, precision styling, and customized organic hair treatments. We feature high-aesthetic styling stations, personalized consultations, and advanced hard-water filtration to deliver a superior client experience.

Avoid being positioned as: A discount quick-cut franchise, a generic hair salon, or an impersonal high-volume chain.

"Artistry in Hair. Bespoke styling and color in a sanctuary of luxury."

Core Customer Promise: Exceptional customized hair design, personalized color artistry, and premium water-softened hair care treatments.

Market Fit: Perth's affluent suburbs have a high disposable income and spend heavily on personal wellness. However, residents are dissatisfied with the noisy, rushed environment of existing salons. Our ground-floor premium boutique model in Subiaco/CBD addresses this gap by offering a quiet, intimate, and highly tailored luxury experience with expert colorists and stylists.

3. Market Context

  • Demographics: Concentrated wealth in premium lifestyle suburbs. High concentration of corporate professionals, wellness enthusiasts, and young families.
  • Spending Power: High local purchasing power. Residents are accustomed to paying AUD 150-250 for premium hair coloring and styling, making AUD 225 (USD 150) for premium salon services highly competitive.
  • Environmental Drivers: Exposure to UV rays, salt water (coastal lifestyles), and hard water in suburban supply networks leads to scalp irritation, dryness, and product buildup, highlighting the need for restorative color treatments.
  • Regulatory Context: Compliance with Australian standards (AS/NZS 3500 plumbing standards), local council commercial zoning, and standard hair and beauty awards (Fair Work) is mandatory.
  • Foreign Corporate Structure: 100% foreign ownership of Australian Pty Ltd companies is allowed, but at least one director must ordinarily reside in Australia.
  • Legality & Dispute Resolution: High asset protection under ASIC. Unfranked dividend remittance is subject to a 30% withholding tax (WHT) unless reduced under tax treaties (typically to 15%).
  • Market Gap: High demand for premium personalized color and cut styling in a private, low-sensory environment, with current premium options being overcrowded and noisy.

10. Customer Journey

  1. Booking: Seamless booking via Instagram / website, integrated with SMS notifications.
  2. Lobby Welcome: Welcome tea, brief intake form regarding hair/scalp concerns.
  3. Consultation: Professional analysis and color/style recommendation.
  4. Treatment: Transition to premium styling station. High-touch service, quiet environment.
  5. Finish & Retail: Final styling check, product recommendation, membership presentation.

11. Setup & Layout

The 800 sq ft space is optimized for visual flow, sensory relaxation, and strict plumbing efficiency:

  • Reception & Retail Lounge (150 sqft): Welcoming entry, retail display, organic tea bar.
  • 4 Premium Styling Stations (400 sqft): Premium styling stations with custom mirrors and optimal spacing for client comfort.
  • 2 Backwash Beds (150 sqft): Backwash stations with integrated wash sinks and professional water carbonation systems.
  • Utility, Color Lab & Staff Room (100 sqft): Hair color mixing station, water heating, laundry, staff rest area.

12. Staffing Model

Staffing models reflect Australian award compliance (MA000005) and standard loaded employer oncosts:

  • 1 Art Director / Master Stylist: USD 5,300/month base loaded (premium styling, operations, training).
  • 1 Senior Stylist: USD 4,400/month base loaded (high-end color and cutting specialist).
  • 1 Part-Time Assistant: USD 2,400/month base loaded (shampooing, laundry, general support).
Incentive Model: 8% commission on membership package sales to motivate staff and secure high-retention bookings.

17. Marketing Strategy

To acquire clients in the premium Perth market, we will prioritize localized channels:

  • Hyper-Local Social Ads: Instagram/TikTok video reels showing the styling transformations and premium color work in action, targeting local premium zip codes.
  • Local Business Cross-Promotions: Partnerships with premium fashion boutiques, wellness/Pilates studios, and luxury cosmetic retailers in Subiaco.
  • Google Maps Optimization: Proactive campaign during the soft launch to secure 5-star reviews specifically referencing the "attentive consultations," "expert styling," and "private salon environment."

Resident Director Retainer & Dividend Withholding Tax Medium ASIC compliance requires an Australian resident nominee director, and unfranked dividends face 30% WHT. Mitigation: Appoint a nominee director via licensed firms and structure transfer pricing to minimize unfranked dividends.
